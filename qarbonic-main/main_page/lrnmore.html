What is carbon offsetting?

Carbon offsetting occurs when a polluting company buys a carbon credit from companies with a negative carbon footprint to make up for the greenhouse gas it has emitted. Offsetting, if done properly, can help channel funds to conservation and sustainable development projects that will reduce emissions, giving companies time to work towards zero emissions. A carbon credit is a kind of tradable permit that, per United Nations standards, equals one tonne of carbon dioxide removed, reduced, or sequestered from the atmosphere. Carbon allowances or caps, meanwhile, are determined by countries or governments according to their emission reduction targets which industries are required to follow.

 

 

What is Qarbonic?

Qarbonic is a Decentralized Application (DApp) that enables the P2P trade of carbon credits (carbon offsets) between companies. We provide a platform for companies to purchase carbon credits digitally that translate to government issued carbon credits which they can use to offset their emissions and stay carbon neutral or to reach their government-set emission caps. We also provide a platform for NGOs to sell their carbon credits for money to earn funding for their operations to conserve the environment.
